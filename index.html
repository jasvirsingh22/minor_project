What are the different types of car insurance policies in India?

Jump Tag Icon Different types of car insurance policies might be marketed via different names, but at their core they offer the following foundational coverages.

1. Third-party Liability Policy

A Third-party Car Insurance Policy is mandatory according to The Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. Thus, you cannot drive a car legally on Indian public roads without buying at least this policy. Why? Because it offers coverage against third-party liabilities like injury, death, and property damage.

2. Own Damage Car Insurance

While your Third-party Car Insurance Policy helps you in case of third-party liabilities, it does not offer any assistance in case your car gets damaged. For that, you need an Own Damage (OD) cover. It is focused on insuring your car against damages arising from accidents, calamities, etc.

3. Comprehensive Car Insurance Policy

A Comprehensive Car Insurance Policy merges the benefits of a Third-party Policy and an Own Damage cover making it a must-have. Here, you can also opt for suitable add-ons to enhance your coverage.

How to choose a four-wheeler insurance policy online

Choosing a car insurance plan that suits and fits your needs is crucial to ensure appropriate coverage and value for money. You should consider the following points while buying Comprehensive Insurance for your four-wheeler.

1. Assess your requirements

Before you go online and purchase an insurance policy, assess your requirements depending on the type of vehicle you own, where you drive it, and the kind of risks you expect to be exposed to. This will help you choose the right plan without spending too much on unnecessary coverages.

2. Compare car insurance plans

One of the main advantages of purchasing four-wheeler insurance online is the ability to compare different policies easily. You can get free car insurance quotes from insurers’ websites, compare the policies based on the quote, features, and services, and then pick the right one as per your requirements.

3. Check the IDV

IDV is the approximate market value of your vehicle and the maximum amount you will receive from the insurance provider if your car suffers a total loss or is damaged beyond repair. Hence, it’s a crucial component to check before making the policy purchase decision. Also, note that a higher IDV offers better insurance protection, but it increases the premium.

4. Check for add-on covers

Add-ons are extra covers that enhance the policy’s coverage. Check for such covers while buying a four-wheeler policy. You can purchase add-ons online by paying an additional premium. But be mindful when buying such covers and opt for only those add-ons that you need.

5. Check the claim process

Choose an insurance provider that offers a simple, quick and hassle-free claim process. You can browse through online reviews to get a fair idea about an insurer’s claim process. Also, do not forget to check the CSR (Claim Settlement Ratio), as the ratio depicts the insurance company’s record when it comes to settling claims.
